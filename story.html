The Kintamani Story

From volcanic highlands to your perfect morning ritual

High in the volcanic highlands of Bali, where morning mists dance across ancient calderas and rich volcanic soil nurtures some of Indonesia's finest arabica beans, lies the source of our signature coffee - Kintamani.

A Coffee Born from Fire

The story of Kintamani coffee begins with Mount Batur, an active volcano that has shaped not just the landscape but the very character of the coffee grown on its slopes. At elevations between 1,200 and 1,600 meters above sea level, the cool mountain air and mineral-rich volcanic soil create ideal conditions for growing exceptional arabica coffee.

What makes our Kintamani coffee truly special is the traditional Balinese farming system called "Subak Abian" - a cooperative approach that has been practiced for centuries. This system, based on the Hindu philosophy of "Tri Hita Karana" (three causes of happiness), emphasizes harmony among people, nature, and the divine.

The Art of Processing

Our Kintamani coffee undergoes a unique wet-hulling process known locally as "Giling Basah." This traditional Indonesian method was developed specifically to handle the country's humid climate and results in the distinctive earthy, herbal notes that Indonesian coffees are famous for.

After careful hand-picking, the cherries are pulped and fermented overnight. The beans are then washed and partially dried to about 30-35% moisture content before the parchment is removed. This early hulling, while the beans are still relatively wet, creates the characteristic blue-green color and full body that defines our Kintamani coffee.

We roast our beans to a medium profile, carefully developing the natural sweetness while preserving the bright, citrusy notes that come from the high altitude. The result is a complex cup with notes of dark chocolate, orange zest, and subtle herbs - a true reflection of its volcanic origins.

Sustainability & Community

At Miel.Bali, we believe that great coffee should benefit everyone in the supply chain. We work directly with three family farms in the Kintamani region, ensuring fair prices that allow farmers to invest in their land and communities. Our direct trade relationships mean we pay 25% above market price, guaranteeing sustainable livelihoods for the families who grow our coffee.

Beyond economics, we're committed to environmental sustainability. All our Kintamani coffee is grown using traditional organic methods, shade-grown under native trees that provide habitat for local birds and wildlife. The farmers we partner with use only natural fertilizers made from coffee pulp and other organic materials, maintaining the health of the volcanic soil for future generations.

Every cup of Kintamani coffee you enjoy at Miel.Bali supports not just exceptional flavor, but also the preservation of traditional farming methods, the protection of Bali's unique ecosystem, and the prosperity of local farming families.
